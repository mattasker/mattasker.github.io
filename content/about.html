About

Hello there! My name is Matthew Asker and I am a theoretical physicist with a strong interest in evolution. I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Theoretical Biology group at the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Biology, located in the beautiful German town of Plön (see header image). On these pages, you can find some information about me and my research.

I grew up in Suffolk, a green county in the east of England, best known for farmland, Ed Sheeran, and my football team, Ipswich Town. After undergraduate studies in theoretical physics at Manchester, I obtained my PhD in Applied Mathematics at Leeds under the supervision of Prof. Mauro Mobilia and Prof. Alastair Rucklidge. During my time in Leeds, I worked on several models of competition in populations under time-varying environments, using tools from across non-equilbrium statistical physics. My thesis can be found here and the individual publications are available on this page.

Since 2025, I have been working as a postdoctoral researcher with Prof. Arne Traulsen. Here, I am working on theoretical models of stress-induced mutagenesis, investigating how stress can lead to increased mutation rates in biological systems. You can read more about this work (soon) and my past research on this page.

In my free time, I enjoy running in nature, playing & watching football, and reading to combat my pathological case of tsundoku. In the summer, you'll also find me taking advantage of the many beautiful lakes in Plön.
